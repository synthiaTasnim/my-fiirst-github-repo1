Centralisation and Decentralisation

Centralisation and decentralisation both are the pattern of authority across wide range/variety of department and position.
Decentralisation is the process of delegating authority throughout the organisation to the middle managers and low level managers.
Centralisation is the process of retaining authority and power to the higher level of management.
Under what circumstances you would work in both centralised and decentralised organisation The circumstances that help one to understand whether to work in a centralised or a deceentralised environment includes :

  • External environment
  • History of the organisation
  • Nature of decision
  • Ability of low level managers
External environment: The greater the uncertainty and complexity of the environment the higher the tendency to to prefer decentralisation because complex and sensitive work environment requires specialists and expert people to remain involved in the process of decision making.so for such case decentralised destribution of authority is preferable.
History of organisation:organisation have the tendency o do what they have done in the past.So,there is relationship between what the organisation have done in its early history and what it will do in terms of Centralisation or decentralisation.
Qualification of lower level managers:If the lower level managers are well enough qualified to take dicision on behalf of their supervisors then the top level managers would take advantage of the suboerdinates' talent and go for decentralisation.
Nature of decision: The costlier and riskier the decision is the more pressure an individual feels to work in a centralised environment.
